Caesar Salad Recipe

Ingredients

  • Romaine lettuce (about 4 cups, chopped)
  • Croutons (1 cup)
  • Parmesan cheese (½ cup, grated)
  • Caesar dressing (⅓ cup)
  • Olive oil (2 tbsp, for drizzling on croutons)
  • Garlic (1 clove, minced)
  • Freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • Lemon juice (optional, for extra flavor)

Execution

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Toss your croutons with olive oil and minced garlic, then spread them on a baking sheet. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until golden brown.

  2. While the croutons are baking, wash and chop the romaine lettuce. Place it in a large bowl.

  3. Once the croutons are ready, add them to the bowl of lettuce along with the grated Parmesan cheese.

  4. Drizzle the Caesar dressing over the salad and toss everything together until well coated.

  5. Finish with a sprinkle of freshly ground black pepper. Optionally, add a squeeze of lemon juice for extra tang.

  6. Serve immediately and enjoy!

For the Ceaser dressing

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice (freshly squeezed)
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon anchovy paste (optional, but adds authentic flavor)
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese (freshly grated)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup olive oil

Execution

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, red wine vinegar, minced garlic, Worcestershire sauce, and anchovy paste (if using).
  2. Whisk the ingredients together until smooth and well combined.
  3. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil while continuously whisking to emulsify the dressing.
  4. Once the dressing is fully combined, stir in the freshly grated Parmesan cheese.
  5. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Adjust the lemon juice or vinegar if you'd like a more tangy flavor.
  6. Store the dressing in the fridge for at least 30 minutes to let the flavors meld together.
  7. Once ready, use the dressing on your Caesar salad or store it for later use (up to 1 week in the fridge).

Key Tips

  • Make your own croutons: For extra flavor, make your own croutons by toasting bread cubes with garlic, olive oil, and a pinch of salt.
  • Use fresh Parmesan: Freshly grated Parmesan has a more intense and authentic flavor compared to pre-grated cheese.
  • Chill your salad bowl: For a refreshing touch, chill your salad bowl in the fridge before mixing the salad, keeping everything crisp and cool.
