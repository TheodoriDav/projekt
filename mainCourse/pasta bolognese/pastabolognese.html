Pasta Bolognese Recipe

Important message follows:** The instructions below are not required to be followed exactly and it is a personally preference, this is only my personal opinion, you should always try to creat, improvise and use your imagination to create something unique just like you are.

Ingredients:

  • Ground beef - 400g
  • Onion (finely chopped) - 1
  • Garlic (minced) - 2 cloves
  • Carrot (finely chopped) - 1
  • Celery stalk (finely chopped) - 1
  • Canned tomatoes - 400g
  • Tomato paste - 2 tbsp
  • Red wine - 1/2 cup
  • Beef broth - 1 cup
  • Dried oregano - 1 tsp
  • Dried basil - 1 tsp
  • Bay leaf - 1
  • Salt and pepper - to taste
  • Olive oil - for cooking
  • Pasta (e.g., tagliatelle or spaghetti) - 400g
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional) - to taste

Execution:

  1. Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion, carrot, and celery, and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the ground beef to the pan, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook until browned.
  5. Pour in the red wine, scraping the bottom of the pan to release any flavorful bits. Let it simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add the canned tomatoes, tomato paste, beef broth, oregano, basil, and bay leaf. Stir to combine.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Reduce the heat and simmer for 30-45 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.
  8. Meanwhile, cook the pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  9. Once the sauce is ready, remove the bay leaf and serve the sauce over the cooked pasta.
  10. Top with grated Parmesan cheese if desired. Serve and enjoy!

  • For a richer flavor, let the sauce simmer for longerâ€”up to 1 hour if you have time.
  • Use a mix of ground beef and pork for a more authentic, flavorful Bolognese sauce.
  • Always cook the pasta al dente and mix it with the sauce, allowing the pasta to absorb the flavors.
