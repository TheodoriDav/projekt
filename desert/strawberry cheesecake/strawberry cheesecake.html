Strawberry Cheesecake Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 3 packages (8 oz each) cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, sliced
  • 1/4 cup strawberry jam or preserves

Execution

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C). Grease a 9-inch springform pan.

  2. In a medium bowl, mix the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Press this mixture into the bottom of the springform pan to form the crust.
  3. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then remove it from the oven and let it cool while you prepare the filling.

  4. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese, sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy.

  5. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition until fully combined.

  6. Pour the cream cheese mixture into the cooled crust and smooth the top.

  7. Bake for 50-55 minutes, or until the center is set and only slightly jiggly. Turn off the oven and let the cheesecake cool in the oven for 1 hour.
  8. After cooling, refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours or overnight to set.

  9. Before serving, heat the strawberry jam in a small saucepan over low heat until melted. Stir in the fresh strawberries.
  10. Spread the strawberry mixture over the top of the cheesecake just before serving.

Key Tips

  • Use room-temperature cream cheese: This ensures a smooth and creamy filling with no lumps.
  • Let the cheesecake cool slowly: Turning off the oven and leaving the door slightly open helps prevent cracks.
  • Chill for best results: Refrigerate overnight for the best texture and flavor.
