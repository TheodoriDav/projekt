Lasagne Recipe

Important message follows:** The instructions below are not required to be followed exactly and it is a personally preference, this is only my personal opinion, you should always try to creat, improvise and use your imagination to create something unique just like you are.

Ingredients:

  • Lasagna noodles - 12 sheets
  • Ground beef - 500g
  • Onion (finely chopped) - 1
  • Garlic (minced) - 2 cloves
  • Canned tomatoes - 800g
  • Tomato paste - 2 tbsp
  • Olive oil - for cooking
  • Dried basil - 1 tsp
  • Dried oregano - 1 tsp
  • Salt and pepper - to taste
  • Ricotta cheese - 400g
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese (sliced) - 200g
  • Parmesan cheese (grated) - 100g
  • Egg - 1
  • Bechamel sauce (or white sauce) - 2 cups

Execution:

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened.
  4. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute. Then add the ground beef and cook until browned.
  5. Stir in the canned tomatoes, tomato paste, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.
  6. In a separate bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, and half of the grated Parmesan. Stir until smooth.
  7. To assemble the lasagna, spread a layer of the meat sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.
  8. Place a layer of cooked lasagna noodles over the sauce, followed by a layer of ricotta mixture, then a layer of mozzarella slices.
  9. Repeat the layers, ending with a layer of meat sauce on top. Pour the bechamel sauce over the top and sprinkle with the remaining Parmesan cheese.
  10. Cover the lasagna with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly.
  11. Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before serving. Enjoy!

Key Tips

  • For an extra creamy texture, add a layer of bechamel sauce between each layer of noodles.
  • Make the meat sauce ahead of time and let it simmer for a few hours to develop a deeper flavor.
  • Cover the lasagna with foil during the first half of baking to prevent it from drying out, then uncover to allow the top to brown.
